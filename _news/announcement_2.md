Placed 5th in rocketry competition and was invited to join NASA's studnet launch initiative (NASA SLI).

---

Our payload consisted of four marshmallows, which expanded due to the lower pressure at our rocket’s altitude. We covered three marshmallow each in different fabric and and had one control marshmellow that did not have any materail surrounding it. We placed each one in a clear, polycarbonate tube and placed distance sensors at the end of each pipe to measure how much the marshmallows expanded down the tubes. In order to effectively cover each marshmallow in the fabric, we sewed mini-spacesuit pouches that were specifically tailored to each marshmallow in order to cover them fully. We had one control marshmallow that had no protective layer; one marshmallow only had a simple cotton covering to prevent it from expanding; one marshmallow had a layer of nylon, and one had a layer of dacron, which are the protective layers used in the Russian and American space suits, respectively. We used this payload to compare the effectiveness of the space suits used by NASA and the Russian space agency, while also comparing it to a simple textile such as cotton.
