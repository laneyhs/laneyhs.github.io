The aim for this project is to get the engineering department at ETHS to recycle 3D printing filament through creating new filament at ETHS. - The goal of this project is to reduce the amount of filament going into the landfill by recycling used filament on site. 

At ETHS, there are many engineering classes that use 3D printing such as Introduction to Engineering, Personal Finance and Engineering
Design. Additionally, many clubs use the 3D printing machines such as Architecture and Engineering Club and Women in Engineering. Learning
how to create 3D models and turning those models into actual tangible objects are quintessential to engineering curriculum and learning.
While this is true, it is also true that 3D printing causes lots of plastic waste. This is because filament is made out of plastic.
Typically, more than 33% of a 3D print is waste, by mass. In one of the engineering classrooms, A164, we have already collected used
filament.

All of this filament currently goes into the landfill which breaks down into microplastics. These microplastics only get broken down into
smaller and smaller plastics but never go away. Additionally, microplastics easily get into the soil, waterways, flora, fauna, and
eventually in humans. There are two main ways to go about recycling filament. The first is to ship off filament to a recycling facility.
This is very costly and does not offer anything in return other than simply recycling our filament. The other option is to recycle used
filament at ETHS into new filament that is able to create 3D prints. This would allow for ETHS engineering students to see how filament is
made and to learn about the environmental side of engineering. 

This project would allow ETHS to have on-site 3D filament recycling into new, usable filament to reuse in 3D printers. We would be able to have different bins labeled to differentiate the kinds of filament next to the 3D printers in room A164 and in room A155 to allow for streamline recycling. The filament recycler machine would be able to be housed in the metal shop underneath the stairs to keep out of the way and to allow for the most ventilation as possible. Additionally, the ETHS community would be able to put their waste 3D filament into bins to recycle their filament.
